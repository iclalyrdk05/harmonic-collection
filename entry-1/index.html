Turkish Tea: A Symbol of Conversation




Box Separation Turkish Tea Culture

In Turkish culture, tea (çay) is more than just a drink; it symbolizes connection and hospitality. Whether welcoming guests, after a meal, watching TV in the evening, or playing games with friends, tea is ever-present in Turkish social gatherings. It plays a key role in breakfast, often serving as a unifying element for families. After meals, a large pot of tea is brewed, and for hours, it is slowly sipped, cup by cup, by everyone in the household. If guests are present

In the tea culture, a few essential accessories accompany the traditional preparation process. A double-stacked teapot, called a "çaydanlık," is used to boil water and brew the tea before serving it. Tea is typically served in small, tulip-shaped glasses, which help concentrate the flavor and retain heat, allowing the aromatic experience to last longer compared to regular cups. Both the çaydanlık and the tea glasses can be made from materials like copper, stainless steel, or glass. Turkish tea drinkers are particular about their utensils, as the quality of these materials can influence the tea’s flavor. Copper teapots are especially valued for their even heating properties and classic aesthetic, deeply rooted in tradition.
