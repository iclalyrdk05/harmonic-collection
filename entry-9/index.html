Ayran: A Guide to Turkey’s Refreshing Yogurt Drink

A deep dive into the culture and traditions of Ayran

What’s Ayran?

Ayran is a traditional yogurt-based drink from Turkey. Known for its creamy texture and slightly salty taste, it's a staple refreshment, especially enjoyed in the warmer months.

History & Origins

Ayran has roots in ancient Turkish nomadic culture, where it was created as a natural thirst-quencher and nutrient source for people on the move.

Recipes & Variations

While traditional Ayran is made with water, yogurt, and salt, you can find regional variations across Turkey and the Middle East that add herbs or different levels of saltiness.

Health Benefits

Ayran is rich in probiotics, calcium, and protein, making it not only refreshing but also beneficial for digestive health.

Serving Traditions

Ayran is typically served cold, often in copper or clay mugs to enhance the taste. It pairs well with dishes like kebabs and pilaf.
